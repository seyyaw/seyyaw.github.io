Short intro:
=====
**Seid Muhie Yimam, Ph.D.**, is the **Technical Lead** at the **[Hub of Computing and Data Science (HCDS)](https://www.hcds.uni-hamburg.de/en/hcds.html)** and a **Research Associate** in the **[Language Technology Group](https://www.inf.uni-hamburg.de/en/inst/ab/lt/home.html)** at the University of Hamburg. He also serves as a **Visiting Professor** at the Instituto Politécnico Nacional (IPN) in Mexico and maintains active research collaborations with several **Ethiopian universities**, where he contributes to PhD student supervision, course teaching, and joint research activities. His research spans **Natural Language Processing**, **artificial intelligence**, and **data science**, with a particular emphasis on **low-resource languages**, **multilingual and multimodal NLP**, **explainable and responsible AI**, and **AI-driven innovation for social impact**, including health care applications.

Dr. Yimam is a member of **Masakhane** and has played an active role in advancing **African NLP** through community-driven research and collaboration. His work includes leading several **SemEval shared tasks**, publishing in major **ACL venues**, and contributing to award-winning research at the forefront of low-resource and multilingual NLP. He received his **Ph.D. in Computer Science** from the University of Hamburg, and his doctoral thesis received the **GSCL Award for Best Doctoral Thesis**. He has also been recognized with **Best Paper Awards**, including the **Deep Learning Indaba Best Paper Award** and the **SemEval 2025 Best Task Paper Award**. Through his sustained leadership in research, teaching, and international collaboration, Dr. Yimam contributes to the application and integration of AI across multiple domains, with a special focus on low-resource settings and languages.
