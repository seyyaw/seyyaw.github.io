        <a href="https://docs.afriannotate.org/">AfriAnnotate &mdash; Data Annotation for African Languages</a>
      </h3>
      <p class="listing-project-desc">
        A collaborative, browser-based annotation platform built for African NLP. It handles
        non-Latin writing systems &mdash; Ge'ez, Tifinagh and right-to-left Arabic script &mdash;
        with proper bidirectional rendering, and supports dataset import from the Hugging Face
        Hub, team and role management, review and quality-control workflows, mobile annotation
        and an API/SDK.
        See the <a href="https://docs.afriannotate.org/">documentation</a>.
      </p>

        <a href="https://masakhanehubtoolingproject.github.io/playbook/">AfriPlaybook &mdash; A Practical Guide for Building NLP Systems for African Languages</a>
      </h3>
      <p class="listing-project-meta">Maintained by the Waraka community with AfricaNLP and Masakhane</p>
      <p class="listing-project-desc">
        A step-by-step manual for building high-quality datasets for African languages, covering
        the full lifecycle: project management, data collection, governance, annotation design,
        quality control, community collaboration and release &mdash; across text, speech, vision
        and multimodal data. Written for volunteers, students, community organisers and
        researchers working with limited resources and multilingual teams.
        Presented as a <a href="/talks/2026-03-26-africanlp-bof/">Birds-of-a-Feather session at EACL 2026</a>.
      </p>

      <p class="listing-project-meta">
        <a href="https://www.hcds.uni-hamburg.de/en/news/20231012-inclusiveai-google.html">Google Award for Inclusion Research</a>
        <span class="listing-meta-sep">&middot;</span> US$60,000
        <span class="listing-meta-sep">&middot;</span> Role: Project Manager
      </p>
      <p class="listing-project-desc">
        A collaboration between HCDS, Universität Hamburg and the Department of Journalism and
        Communications, Bahir Dar University, examining how social media intensifies armed
        conflict while silencing marginalised voices &mdash; particularly women affected by
        conflict-related sexual violence &mdash; and building inclusive technologies that
        strengthen women's participation in peacebuilding.
        Outputs include <a href="https://aclanthology.org/2026.findings-acl.1433/">POLAR</a>,
        a benchmark for multilingual, multicultural and multi-event online polarization across
        22 languages (<a href="/files/POLAR-EEUCA-2026.pdf">presentation</a>) &mdash; Best Paper
        Award at EEUCA 2026 @ ACL 2026 &mdash; which also ran as
        <a href="https://www.hcds.uni-hamburg.de/en/news/2025/20250905-sharedtasks.html">SemEval&#8209;2026 Task&nbsp;9</a>,
